GST TAX SLABS:
- 0% Rate (Exempted): Essential items like fresh milk, curd, vegetables, bread, salt.
- 5% Rate: Household necessities like sugar, spices, tea, coffee, edible oil.
- 12% Rate: Processed foods, computers, mobiles.
- 18% Rate: Capital goods, soap, toothpaste, hair oil, industrial intermediates.
- 28% Rate: Luxury items like cars, high-end motorcycles, tobacco, aerated drinks.

IGST VS CGST/SGST LOGIC:
- Intra-State Supply (location of supplier and place of supply are in same state): Apply CGST + SGST.
   - CGST = GST Rate / 2
   - SGST = GST Rate / 2
- Inter-State Supply (location of supplier and place of supply are in different states): Apply IGST.
   - IGST = GST Rate
- Union Territories: Apply UTGST instead of SGST.

INPUT TAX CREDIT (ITC):
- Businesses can claim ITC on GST paid on purchases to offset GST collected on sales.
- Blocked Credits (Section 17(5)): No ITC on food & beverages, personal use items, membership of clubs.

REGISTRATION THRESHOLDS:
- Goods: 40 Lakhs turnover (20 Lakhs for special category states).
- Services: 20 Lakhs turnover (10 Lakhs for special category states).

E-WAY BILL:
- Required for movement of goods worth > Rs. 50,000.
- Inter-state: Mandatory.
- Intra-state: Defined by state rules.

INVOICE RULES:
- Tax Invoice: Issued by registered person for taxable supplies.
- Bill of Supply: Issued for exempt supplies or by composition dealers.
- Mandatory fields: GSTIN, Invoice No, Date, HSN/SAC, Taxable Value, Tax Rate, Place of Supply.
